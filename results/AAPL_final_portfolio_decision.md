### Synthesis of the Debate

The risk analysts presented three distinct perspectives, but the weight of evidence – both technical and fundamental – supports a cautious reduction in AAPL exposure.

Sticky rates, rising oil prices from Iran conflict, and regulatory risks (EU DMA, DOJ) pose direct threats to iPhone demand and Services margins. The bear’s stop-loss logic ($260, the 50-day SMA) is a clean technical level that respects volatility (2× ATR).

- Over-reliance on a single record quarter and dismissal of seasonality.  
- Mischaracterization of the MACD histogram narrowing and RSI divergence as “healthy cooldowns” – they are early warning signals of trend exhaustion.  
- A “tighten stop to $255” recommendation that straddles the no-man’s land between the 50- and 200-day SMAs, inviting gap-fill risk.

**The Neutral Analyst’s “trim 20% with tiered stops” is reasonable but less decisive** – selling 20% still leaves 80% exposed to a 5–10% correction that the bearish divergences and macro headwinds make probable.
